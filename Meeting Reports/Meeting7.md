### **Discussion :**
During this meeting, 3 main topics were discussed: branching convention, viusal identity of our website, the personality quiz.  

Indeed, when reviewing the meeting report6, we first discussed the branch naming convention that will be used for hte project. We decide to use a clear and consistent format for our Git branches to have a better collaboration and organization.  
We agreed on these following rules : 
- **feature/** or **feat/** for new features, 
- **bugfix/** for bug corrections, 
- **hotfix/** for urgent fixes on the production branch, 
- **release/** for preparing a new version, 
- **docs/** for documentation updates (such as the README file and the meeting reports), 
- **test/** for experiments outside of a specific issue.  

We then talked about the visual identity of our website. We decided that we could see to include both dark and light modes. We also kept a placeholder font for now *"Trebuchet MS (sans-serif)"*

Another important point we talk over was the personality quiz. As the quiz's name will me *"The IT match"*, we thaught about making it a swipe-based quiz, with questions similar to “would you rather” questions where the user chooses between two options by swiping left or right.  
We also reviewed some example questionsthat we had prepared for the quiz. They are based on different personality dimensions such as:
- Logical vs Creative.
- Solo vs Team.
- Theoretical vs Practical.
- Low-level vs High-level.
- Math-oriented vs Intuition-driven.

Where some example questions could be :
- When organizing a party, do you prefer making a detailed checklist or brainstorming wild ideas?
- When facing a tricky problem, do you follow a proven method or experiment until something works?
- In a project, do you prefer working alone or collaborating with others?
- When learning something new, do you prefer a solo tutorial or a hands-on workshop?
- When studying, do you prefer understanding why something works or learning directly how to use it?
